Arnold Palmer

"The King"

Timeline

  • 1929 - born in Latrobe, PA
  • 1933 - swings club for first time
  • 1946 - wins first of five West Penn Amateur Championships
  • 1947 - arrives at Wake Forest University
  • 1949 - Wins first of two NCAA individual championships
  • 1951 - Enlists in US Coast Guard
  • 1954 - Wins US Amateur at Country Club of Detroit
  • Wins first PGA tour event at the Canadian Open
  • 1958 - Wins the Masters for first time
  • 1960 - Wins second Masters
  • 1960 - Wins US Open at Cherry Hills
  • 1961 - Wins Open Championship at Royal Birkdale
  • 1962 - Wins third Masters
  • 1962 - Wins second Open Championship
  • 1963 - Leads US to Ryder Cup victory
  • 1964 - Wins fourth Masters
  • 1968 - First to surpasses $1 million in earnings
  • 1970 - Named "Athlete of the decade"
  • 1973 - Caps off his 62 PGA Tour victories
  • 1976 - Purchases Bay Hill & Lodge
  • 1980 - Wins PGA Seniors' Championship in first start
  • 1981 - Wins US Senior Open
  • 1989 - Arnold Palmer Children's Hospital opens in Orlando, FL
  • 1994 - Plays last US Open at Oakmont Country Club
  • 1995 - Plays last Open Championship
  • 2004 - 50th consecutive Masters start
  • 2004 - Awarded Medal of Freedom from President George W. Bush
  • 2007 - Becomes Masters honorary starter
  • 2016 - Dies at age of 87
